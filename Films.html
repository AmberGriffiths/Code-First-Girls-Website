Festive Films

How the Grinch stole Christmas (2000)

On a tiny snowflake floating through the air, exists the town of Who-ville, home of the Whos. In the nearby mountain lives the grinch, a mean green monster with a hatred for Christmas. The Grinch has a plan to ruin Christmas for the citizens of Whoville, but his plan is foiled by a young Who named Cindy Lou Who. She makes the grinch realise the joys of Christmas through her kindness and manages to save Christmas for Who-ville. Based off the loved Children's story written by Dr. Seuss.

The Polar Express (2004)

A magical train heading to the north pole, the polar express only appears to those who still believe in Christmas. Taking a magical ride full of adventure, a young boy's belief in Christmas is challenged. Many complications occur on the journey, from ice on the tracks to a child losing his ticket... But through his new-found friends and his night of exploration, the boy sees that the wonder of Christmas never fades for those who truly believe.

Home Alone (1990)

When Kevin McAllister is left behind when his family go on holiday, Christmas comes early for the 8-year old who takes on Chicago by himself. From eating and watching whatever he wants to taking over his house, Kevin thinks nothing can stop him. However, when two burglars notice that he seems to be alone, they begin to plot the ultimate break in. But after a failed attempt, Kevin lays booby traps and awaits their arrival. Can they really be outsmarted by an 8-year old?

Love Actually (2003)

A collection of intertwining stories that are guaranteed to warm your heart, Love Actually is a must-see Christmas film. How will the new Prime Minister fair with his new secretory who he has feelings for? Will Sarah confess to Carl how she feels? Does Colin actually find love in America? And will 'Christmas Is All Around' give Billy Mack the no.1 chart spot?
